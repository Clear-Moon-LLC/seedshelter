Frequently Asked Questions

Honest answers to the most critical survival gardening questions.

How long do the seeds last in storage?
When kept sealed in our 7-mil Mylar bags with the included oxygen absorbers and stored in a cool, dark place (like a basement), the seeds will remain highly viable for 5 to 10+ years. Once opened, viability gradually decreases over 2 to 3 years.
Can I really feed a family from a bucket of seeds?
Yes, provided you have the space and soil. Our Family Vault requires approximately 1,800 square feet of growing space (roughly a 42x42 foot plot) to yield its full caloric potential. We do not make false claims about feeding a family from a window box.
What USDA zones do these work in?
Our carefully selected heirloom varieties thrive in USDA Zones 3 through 10, covering over 95% of the continental United States. The planting guide included in the vaults shows you exactly when to plant based on your local first and last frost dates.
Are these seeds non-GMO?
Absolutely. 100% of our seeds are non-GMO, open-pollinated heirlooms. This is critical for survival: it means you can save the seeds from your harvest and replant them year after year with the same genetic traits. You never have to buy seeds again.
What if I've never gardened before?
We highly recommend starting with our Go-Bag or 90-Day Starter Kit right now to practice. Our included planting guides are written for beginners, covering everything from seed depth to watering schedules. Survival gardening is a skill you must practice before you truly need it.

The Infinite Harvest

True food security means never relying on a seed company again. Here's how the cycle works.

🌱

1. Plant

Sow your open-pollinated heirloom seeds in well-prepared soil according to our frost date calendar.

🍅

2. Harvest

Enjoy the majority of your crop, but leave the healthiest, strongest plants to fully mature and over-ripen on the vine.

🤲

3. Save

Extract, dry, and store the seeds from those over-ripe fruits. Beans and peas are the easiest; just let the pods dry on the plant!

🔄

4. Replant

Next season, plant those saved seeds. They will grow into the exact same variety, adapted slightly to your local microclimate.
